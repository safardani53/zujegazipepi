13 best patio heaters UK 2023 top infrared, gas and electric heaters for outdoors

ENJOY your outside space, whatever the weather, with the best patio heaters to keep everyone toasty as the sun goes down.

From wall-mounted or hanging heaters to portable or table-top options, there's a wide range of ways to heat up your garden, balcony or patio.

Traditional gas patio heaters can give out a lot of heat over a wide area but are less eco-friendly than electric heaters.

Electric heaters, however, can take longer to heat up and need to be near a power source - plus, they can hike up your energy bills.

Infrared heaters can be a more energy-efficient compromise as they convert almost 100% of the electricity to heat and warm objects and people, rather than just the air next to the heater.

When buying your patio heater check the coverage (in square feet) and the BTUs, which stand for British Thermal Units. The larger the BTU rating, the larger the heat radius – as a general rule, freestanding patio heaters produce about 40,000 BTUs per hour, which is enough to heat a 20-foot diameter space.

Another option to consider is a fire pit or a chiminea for a more rustic way to warm up your party guests - they also look stylish and are a nice focal point for outside gatherings.

Do patio heaters work?

Yes, patio heaters can be extremely effective as they don't rely on convection and deliver the heat directly to where it's required.

This 'radiant heat' travels straight through the cold air and can keep you nice and toasty on a chilly evening.

Are patio heaters bad for the environment?

Trying to decide on a patio heater is no easy task but working out which one is the most eco-friendly is an even bigger job.

Although no heater will be perfectly eco-friendly there are some which are better than others. For example, Electric heaters are much more green when it comes to their environmental impact. Gas heaters produce Co2, while electrical ones don't.

Some models take a while to heat up, using more resources to do so.

Ultimately, if you put a heater into any garden, whether big or small, you will find that the heat dissipates quickly because of the cold air.

Add some wind to the scenario and it's even harder to heat! The best time to use heaters is when it is not so windy and is a balmy evening already.

What should I look for in a patio heater?

There are a few things to consider, for example check the heat coverage provided by the heater and whether it will work for the size of your outside space. Also, the size of the heater itself is a consideration - making sure it works in your space and can provide an effective but comfortable level of warmth.

You should also think about whether a gas or electric model would work best for you - based on whether you have an outside socket, as well as energy-efficiency and running costs.

How much do patio heaters cost?

There's a huge range of types and costs of patio heaters - from £30 to £500 or more.

However, we found that you can get a good quality patio heater with a good heat coverage for £100-£200.

Are patio heaters expensive to run

How much it costs to run your patio heater will depend on its size, heat output and what setting you run it on, but generally speaking, electric patio heaters are cheaper to run than gas versions.

Following recent price increases, electricity now costs 34p/kWh if you pay by direct debit, so for a patio heater with an output of 2kW that’s going to cost you 68p per hour and for 3kW, £1.02 per hour.

The cheapest patio heaters to run are those that burn wood, such as fire pits and chimineas, especially if you already have a supply of suitable wood lying around, otherwise bags of logs are readily available and the more bags you buy, the cheaper they are.

Should I buy a gas, infrared or electric patio heater?

This is really up to your own personal choice  - gas heaters are more harmful to the environment but electric heaters can increase you energy bills and take longer to warm up - as well as needing to be close to a power outlet. 

Infrared patio heaters are a good option as they are thought to be more energy efficient than electric heaters. This is because they convert almost all of the electricity to heat and warm objects and people close-by rather than the air.

Is it safe to use a patio heater indoors?

Unless stated by the manufacturer, it is best never to use a patio heater indoors. This can be a dangerous fire hazard and if you have children or pets can be highly unsafe around them.

There are a couple of heater brands on the market that can be deemed as indoor and outdoor heaters - but this must be thoroughly checked with the individual company and if in doubt, assume they can't be used inside.

How many patio heaters do I need?

How many patio heaters you will need depends entirely on your space. If you have a patio, one will probably suffice, but this will all depend on your walls and the size of the space.

A garden will probably need more than one if you're having a large party, while if you are just having a cosy dinner for two around a garden table then one will surely suffice.

Where to buy patio heaters

There are many great retailers selling outdoor patio heaters which give thorough information and instructions on what is best to buy when it comes to choosing the best patio heater for your space.

Do patio heaters use a lot of gas?

For gas patio heaters, an average 20lb propane tank would give you around 10 hours of heat on the highest setting.

An electric patio heater will last a lot longer but you're limited to being within range of a plug socket.

Do patio heaters blow over?

Unsecured patio heaters can blow over if the wind is strong enough and it's recommended not to use one in high winds.

The best way to stop your patio heater from tipping over is to secure it to a sturdy structure.
